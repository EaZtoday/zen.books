# Zen Books — Full Deliverables Matrix (All 4 ICPs x 3 Tiers)

> **How to read this document:**
> Each ICP has 3 tiers (Cleanup, Monthly, Premium). Every tier has two columns:
> - **"What You're Doing in QBO"** — the actual tasks performed in QuickBooks Online (for internal use / the bookkeeper)
> - **"What the Client Sees"** — how you describe or frame the deliverable on the website and in sales conversations
>
> This is meant to be your master reference. The website copy pulls from the "Client Sees" column. The internal SOPs and checklists pull from the "QBO Work" column.

## ICP 1: Real Estate Investors

### Tier 1 — Portfolio Cleanup & Setup (One-Time)

**Best for:** Investors who are behind and need a fresh start — or are moving from spreadsheets to QBO for the first time.

| What You're Doing in QBO | What the Client Sees |
|---|---|
| Review and rebuild Chart of Accounts for RE: Rental Income, Property Taxes, Insurance, Mortgage Interest, Repairs & Maintenance, Capital Improvements, Management Fees, etc. | "We build a QuickBooks file that actually makes sense for rental properties." |
| Set up Class tracking (or Location tracking) so every transaction ties to a specific property | "Every dollar is tagged to a property so you can see performance per door." |
| Go through historical bank and credit card feeds; categorize every past transaction to the correct account + correct property class | "We categorize your entire backlog so your history is accurate and usable." |
| Reconcile all bank accounts, credit cards, and mortgage/escrow accounts — month by month, to the penny | "Your bank balances match QBO exactly. No more mystery differences." |
| Fix opening balances, negative balances, or "Uncategorized" buckets that built up | "We clean up the junk data that makes your reports unreliable." |
| Untangle intercompany transfers between LLCs (if multi-entity — each QBO file gets cleaned) | "Transfers between your entities are tracked clearly so nothing falls through the cracks." |
| Separate personal transactions → Owner's Draw / Owner's Equity | "Personal spending is pulled out so your business numbers are accurate." |
| Review and reclassify CapEx vs. Repairs (roof replacement vs. fixing a faucet) | "Capital improvements and repairs are categorized correctly — this matters at tax time." |
| Clean up Undeposited Funds, orphaned journal entries, and duplicate transactions | "We fix the hidden errors that make your P&L wrong." |

**Deliverable to the client at the end:**
- Clean QBO file with all accounts reconciled through the current period
- Property-level P&L (one for each door / class)
- Portfolio-wide P&L summary
- Brief written summary: "Here's what we found and fixed"

| What You're Doing in QBO | What the Client Sees |

## ICP 3: Nonprofits

| What You're Doing in QBO | What the Client Sees |
|---|---|

- Clean QBO file with all accounts reconciled through current period

## Notes for Website Copy

1. **The website only shows the "Client Sees" column.** The QBO work column is your internal checklist and SOP reference.

2. **Keep bullet points to 3-5 per tier on the website.** The tables above are exhaustive — pick the 3-5 most compelling items per tier for the public-facing page.

3. **Industry-specific language matters.** "NOI per door" speaks to an investor. "Food cost %" speaks to a restaurant owner. "Grant burn rate" speaks to an ED. Use their words.

4. **The tiering logic is the same across all ICPs:**
   - Cleanup = one-time, fix the past
   - Monthly = recurring, maintain the present
   - Premium = recurring, plan the future

5. **Restaurants & Construction are combined on one page** but have distinct deliverables. Use headers or tabs to separate them visually (e.g., "For Restaurants" / "For Construction" within each tier).

6. **Nonprofits have unique terminology** — Statement of Activities (not P&L), Statement of Financial Position (not Balance Sheet), functional expense allocation, restricted vs. unrestricted. Use these terms on the nonprofit page to build credibility.
